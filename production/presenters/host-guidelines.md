# NDB-TV Presenter Host Guidelines

## On-Camera Standards

### Delivery
- **Pace:** Moderate and clear. Speak as if explaining to a friend.
- **Tone:** Warm, confident, and respectful.
- **Language:** Hassaniya Arabic for conversational content. MSA for formal content. French where appropriate.
- **Energy:** Match energy to content — energetic for youth topics, calm for educational content.

### Script Rules
- Read the script at least twice before recording.
- Mark natural pauses and emphasis points.
- Do not add unapproved statements, claims, or opinions.
- If you make a mistake, pause, restart the sentence, continue.
- If the script contains a word/phrase you're unsure how to pronounce, ask before recording.

### On-Camera Conduct
- Look directly at the camera lens (not the screen).
- Keep hand movements natural and purposeful.
- Sit/stand straight — professional posture.
- Smile naturally — not forced.

### Branding
- NDB-TV logo must be visible (lower-third or background) throughout.
- Do not wear competitor branding.
- Background should be clean, professional, or contextually relevant.
- Intro: Always begin with "NDB-TV" channel identifier.
- Outro: Always end with call to action + tagline.

## Content Boundaries

| Allowed | Not Allowed |
|---------|-------------|
| Explaining concepts clearly | Giving personal opinion as fact |
| Reading from approved script | Deviating from approved script |
| Expressing excitement about the topic | Making political statements |
| Sharing personal experience (if approved) | Making unverified claims |
| Addressing the audience directly | Speaking negatively about competitors/individuals |

## Audio Standards
- Use a dedicated microphone (not phone/computer built-in mic).
- Avoid rooms with echo or background noise.
- Record at consistent volume — not too quiet, not clipping.
- Submit audio check before first recording session.
