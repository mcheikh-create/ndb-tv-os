# NDB-TV Avatar Style Guide

## Status: Draft — Awaiting Founder Approval

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines a unified visual and behavioral style for all AI-generated avatar presenters on NDB-TV. Avatars must be recognizably synthetic but professional, trustworthy, and culturally appropriate.

## 2. Visual Style

### Appearance
- **Style:** Modern, clean, professional — similar to a news anchor or online educator
- **Background:** Clean studio setting or relevant contextual background
- **Expression:** Neutral-positive. Smiling when appropriate, serious when delivering factual/critical information
- **Eye contact:** Direct-to-camera (simulated via AI)
- **Movement:** Natural head movements, hand gestures where supported — avoid static/t-pose

### Prohibited
- Photorealistic resemblance to any real person
- Unlicensed celebrity/notable person likeness
- Culturally inappropriate attire
- Violent, political, or religious symbology
- Distracting rapid movements or flashing visuals

### Attire (when supported)
- Professional — equivalent to local TV news presenter
- Modest, culturally appropriate for Nouadhibou/Mauritania
- Colors consistent with NDB-TV brand (deep blue, white, red accent)

## 3. Voice Guidelines

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone:** Warm, clear, professional
- **Language:** Arabic (Hassaniya preferred for conversational, MSA for formal), French support
- **Speed:** Moderate — 150-170 words per minute
- **Pitch:** Natural range — not artificially high or low

### Pronunciation
- Hassaniya Arabic with clear articulation
- Proper pronunciation of Nouadhibou place names
- Technical terms (AI, digital, etc.) pronounced correctly in context

## 4. On-Screen Behavior

### Positioning
- Standard: Centered or right-aligned with visuals on opposite side
- Lower-third name bar: "AI Presenter | NDB-TV"

### Transitions
- Avatar appears via clean fade/slide — no jarring pop-ins
- When transitioning between avatar and visuals, use NDB-TV branded transitions

## 5. Content Rules

### What Avatars CAN Present
- Educational tutorials and explainers
- News briefs (factual, sourced)
- Public service announcements
- Cultural and historical content
- Skill-building demonstrations
- Translations/localizations

### What Avatars CANNOT Present
- Political opinions or endorsements
- Religious commentary
- Unverified claims or speculation
- Medical, legal, or financial advice without approved disclaimer
- Content requiring empathy that an AI cannot genuinely convey

## 6. Labeling Requirements

Every avatar video must include:
- **Intro text:** "Presented by [Avatar Name], NDB-TV AI Presenter"
- **Description:** Clear statement that the presenter is AI-generated
- **End screen:** Logo + "AI-Generated Presenter" label

## 7. Approval Process

1. Script written from approved template
2. Script reviewed by founder/editor
3. Avatar selected from approved profiles
4. Video generated
5. Final review before publishing
6. Post-publishing monitoring

## 8. Limitations

- Avatars have no personal experience, emotions, or opinions
- They cannot answer questions in real-time (pre-recorded only)
- They reflect the quality of the script — garbage in, garbage out
- Over-reliance on avatars reduces human connection with the audience
