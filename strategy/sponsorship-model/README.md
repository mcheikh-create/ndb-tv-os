# Sponsorship Model

**Status:** Draft
**Target Activation:** At 2,000+ subscribers (or earlier for non-monetary partnerships)

---

## Philosophy

NDB-TV accepts sponsorship that aligns with its mission and values. Sponsorship is a partnership, not a transaction. Sponsors support the channel's public service mission; they do not buy influence over content.

## Sponsorship Categories

### Tier 1: Community Partner (In-Kind)
- **Value:** Services, equipment, or resources instead of cash
- **Benefits:** Logo in description, social media thank you, community acknowledgment
- **Commitment:** Minimal — no ongoing obligation
- **Ideal for:** Local businesses, NGOs, community organizations

### Tier 2: Content Supporter (Cash — Entry)
- **Investment:** Negotiable
- **Benefits:** Logo on channel banner, mention in 1 video/month, description link
- **Commitment:** Monthly or quarterly
- **Ideal for:** Local businesses aligned with channel themes

### Tier 3: Program Sponsor (Cash — Mid)
- **Investment:** Negotiable (higher than Tier 2)
- **Benefits:** Program-level branding (e.g., "Health First brought to you by X"), logo in program intro, sponsor acknowledgment in each episode, social media posts
- **Commitment:** Quarterly or annual
- **Ideal for:** Companies with CSR budgets, health organizations, educational institutions

### Tier 4: Founding Partner (Cash — Premium)
- **Investment:** Highest tier
- **Benefits:** Named as founding partner, logo on all channel materials, priority association with NDB-TV brand, input on content calendar (not editorial control), first right of refusal on special projects
- **Commitment:** Annual
- **Ideal for:** Major local employers, international organizations with Mauritania programs, telecom companies

---

## Sponsor Guidelines

### Acceptable Sponsors
- Local businesses in Nouadhibou
- Health organizations and clinics
- Educational institutions
- Technology companies (ethical)
- NGOs and development organizations
- Government agencies (non-political)

### Unacceptable Sponsors
- Political parties or candidates
- Tobacco, alcohol, or gambling companies
- Organizations with human rights violations
- Predatory lending or financial services
- Any entity requiring editorial control

---

## Sponsor Onboarding Process

1. **Initial conversation** — Understand sponsor's goals
2. **Alignment check** — Pass mission fit assessment
3. **Package proposal** — Appropriate tier recommendation
4. **Agreement** — Written terms, no editorial influence clause
5. **Integration** — Materials created, schedule set
6. **Delivery** — Benefits delivered per agreement
7. **Reporting** — Metrics shared with sponsor

---

## Disclosure Requirements

- All sponsorship relationships publicly listed on channel
- Sponsored content clearly labeled
- Sponsorship income included in annual transparency report
