# Event Coverage

## Purpose

Coverage of local events — sports, cultural, community, educational — that matter to Nouadhibou.

---

## Coverage Process

### 1. Planning (1–2 weeks before)
- [ ] Event confirmed for coverage
- [ ] Access/permissions arranged
- [ ] Key interviews identified and contacted
- [ ] Shooting plan prepared
- [ ] Equipment reserved
- [ ] Team assigned

### 2. Pre-Event (Day before)
- [ ] Equipment checked and charged
- [ ] Travel plan confirmed
- [ ] Backup plan reviewed
- [ ] Interview subjects confirmed

### 3. On-Site
- [ ] Arrive 30 min early
- [ ] Establish contact with event organizers
- [ ] Begin filming establishing shots
- [ ] Conduct scheduled interviews
- [ ] Capture B-roll throughout
- [ ] Note key moments for short-form extraction

### 4. Post-Event (Within 48 hours)
- [ ] Footage transferred and organized
- [ ] Best moments identified
- [ ] Main recap video edited
- [ ] Shorts extracted (2–3)
- [ ] Photo gallery compiled
- [ ] Published on schedule

---

## Coverage Priorities

### Always Cover
- Major sports events
- Community festivals and celebrations
- Educational events (workshops, graduations)
- Cultural events

### Selectively Cover
- Business openings
- Political events (only as community interest, not partisan)
- Private events (by invitation only, mission-aligned)

### Do Not Cover
- Politically partisan rallies
- Unsafe or high-risk events
- Events that conflict with NDB-TV values

---

## Equipment Kit

### Minimum (1 person)
- Smartphone with good camera
- Portable microphone
- Portable charger

### Standard (2 people)
- Camera + tripod
- External microphone
- Lighting (portable LED)
- Backup recording device

---

## Post-Event Content Package

| Deliverable | Format | Timeline |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Teaser | Short (15–30s) | 24h before event |
| Live updates | Stories/shorts | During event |
| Recap video | 5–10 min | Within 48h |
| Highlight shorts | 2–3 × 30–60s | Within 72h |
| Photo gallery | 10–20 photos | Within 72h |
